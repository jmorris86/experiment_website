Onlinemarket

Overview

You are a purchaser of goods in an online marketplace that consists of three different vendors. The goods sold by each vendor are exactly the same.

Due to delivery issues in the market, orders sometimes arrive in bad condition. If the order arrives in bad condition you will be paid £0. Whereas, you are paid £1 for orders that arrive in good condition.

Vendors are real people who are paid for the number of sales they make that arrive in good condition.

Each vendor is randomly allocated a type, Good and Bad. Products arrive in good condition from Good vendors more often than from Bad vendors. However, at times, products arrive in bad condition from both types of vendor.

Finally, given delivery is, outside the control of the vendors, if a product arrives in good condition in one round, it does not mean it will arrive in good condition in the following round, and vice versa.

Highlights

Your role

  • You are a purchaser in an online marketplace. In each round you will be asked to buy a product from one of several vendors.
  • Each purchase from a vendor generates payoff of £1 or £0.

Vendors

  • Vendors are real people who will be paid a percentage of their total sales that generated a payoff of £1.
  • They have been randomly selected as Good or Bad vendors.
  • Good vendors are more likely to pay £1 than bad vendors.
